We get the word 'Narcissus' from Greek mythology. A nymph called Echo fell in love with a young Greek named Narcissus, but Narcissus told her to leave him alone. Heartbroken, she lived alone until nothing but an echo of her remained. Nemesis, the God of revenge, heard the story and lured Narcissus to a pool. Narcissus, who was very handsome and quite taken with himself, saw his reflection in the pool and, as he leaned over to see better, fell in and drowned. He turned into the flower.

Daffodils were introduced into gardens in about 300BC. The Greek botanist and philosopher Theophrastus listed and described many of the earliest known kinds of narcissus in his nine-volume 'Enquiry into Plants'. Daffodils were brought to Britain by the Romans who thought that the sap from daffodils had healing powers. Actually the sap contains crystals that can irritate the skin.

Daffodils are a sign of prosperity, good luck, and wealth in China, and blossom around Chinese New Year. If these flowers bloom on New Year’s Day, it is a sign of good fortune for the coming year. Daffodils are one of the first flowers to emerge from the snow once the winter has passed. In March or early April, they usually appear. As a result, they are associated with March as a birth month and springtime. Daffodils also symbolise a variety of excellent attributes, including Revival and Rebirth, Hope, Joy, Resilience, Good Luck and Prosperity, Memories, and Forgiveness.
Due to their popularity as cultivated plants, thousands of cultivars have been bred . The perianths (petals) are mostly yellow or white, but can occasionally be orange, green, or red or a combination of these colours. Today, many cultivars have brightly coloured coronas (cups) which may be yellow, white, pink, orange, red, green or a combination of these.

We give flowers to express our love and affection to people important to us. When you give someone daffodils, you can make it more special by knowing the meaning behind every color.

➽ White daffodil represent purity, innocence, and new beginnings.
➽ Yellow daffodil is associated with a variety of good emotions, including happiness, optimism, and prosperity.
➽ Orange daffodil represent bravery, zeal, health, and friendship.
➽ Pink daffodil are often associated with platonic love.
➽ Purple daffodil is often associated with selfishness or self-obsession.
